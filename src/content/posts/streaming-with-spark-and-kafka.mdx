Streaming systems feel simple until something fails.

The parts I pay attention to are the ones that determine whether the pipeline recovers cleanly:

- checkpointing
- consumer offsets
- schema evolution
- lag monitoring

If the ingest path is not designed for replay and operational recovery, the rest of the pipeline is
too expensive to trust.

## What I care about

I want streaming jobs to be:

- easy to restart
- obvious to observe
- cheap enough to run continuously
- predictable enough for downstream teams
